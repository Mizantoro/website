Space Stats

Since you have entered this site...

It has been:

Every one second the sun converts ~4 billion kg of matter into energy. (*)

Voyager 1 travels at a speed of 61,197 km/h, which is around 17 km/s relative to the sun. (*)

Every one century, on average, ~1.68 supernovae take place in a medium-sized (similar to the Milky Way) galaxy. If we take into account the entire observable universe, every one second ~181 stars go supernova! (*)

Every year, on average, ~6.5 stars are formed in a medium-sized galaxy. If we take into account the entire observable universe, every one second around 412,000 stars form! (*)

Light in the void travels at the speed of 299,792,458 m/s

Keep in mind that this website uses approximate data. The purpose of this project is to show the scale of processes in a visual form and is not meant to be used as a source.
